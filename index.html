Aim of the Experiment
objective • scope • learning outcomes

Primary Objective

To study and simulate heat conduction along a metal rod, verify Fourier's Law of Heat Conduction, and analyse steady-state temperature distribution through interactive numerical modelling.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand how thermal energy moves from hot regions to cold regions.
  • Observe the effect of conductivity on heat diffusion speed.
  • Identify steady state and linear temperature distribution.
  • Interpret a temperature graph and heat flux values.
Theory
background equations • concepts

What is Heat Conduction?

Heat conduction is the transfer of thermal energy through matter via direct particle-to-particle interaction without bulk movement of the material. It occurs whenever a temperature gradient exists across a solid or stationary fluid.

Fourier's Law

Q = -kA(dT/dx)

The negative sign indicates that heat flows from high temperature to low temperature. The thermal conductivity k decides how easily a material allows heat to pass through it.

Transient Heat Equation

∂T/∂t = α ∂²T/∂x²

This equation describes how temperature changes with time. In steady state, ∂T/∂t = 0, so the rod temperature becomes linear from the hot end to the cold end.

Finite Difference Idea

The rod is divided into small nodes. Each node exchanges heat with its neighbours step by step, creating a realistic diffusion effect. Larger conductivity means quicker spreading of heat.
